
# Mentions

With the flags `MD_FLAG_MENTION_AT` and/or `MD_FLAG_MENTION_HASH`, MD4C enables
recognition of mention spans.

A mention is written as an indicator character (`@` or `#`) immediately followed
by display text in square brackets and an optional target in parentheses:

    @[display text](target)
    #[display text](target)

The target is free-form — any string up to the closing `)`.

The HTML renderer in md4c-html treats `MD_SPAN_MENTION` as a no-op (the display
text is emitted as normal text).  Application-level renderers use the indicator
and target fields from `MD_SPAN_MENTION_DETAIL` to produce their own output.


## Basic @-mentions (users)

The `@`, `[`, `]`, and `(target)` are all consumed by the parser; only the
display text appears in the output.

```````````````````````````````` example
Hello @[John Doe](user:123)!
.
<p>Hello John Doe!</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Hello @[John Doe](user:123) and @[Jane](user:456).
.
<p>Hello John Doe and Jane.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](user:1) @[Bob](user:2) @[Carol](user:3)
.
<p>Alice Bob Carol</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Mention at the very start of a paragraph.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John](user:42) please review this.
.
<p>John please review this.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Mention at the very end of a paragraph.

```````````````````````````````` example
Please review this @[John](user:42)
.
<p>Please review this John</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Numeric-only display text is allowed.

```````````````````````````````` example
Hey @[007](user:7)!
.
<p>Hey 007!</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Basic #-mentions (channels)

```````````````````````````````` example
Check #[general](channel:42) for updates.
.
<p>Check general for updates.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Post in #[announcements](channel:1) or #[random](channel:2).
.
<p>Post in announcements or random.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
#[engineering](channel:10) #[design](channel:11) #[marketing](channel:12)
.
<p>engineering design marketing</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Role mentions

The target prefix `role:` carries the role type and an optional numeric ID.
Any colon-delimited sub-structure is opaque to the parser.

```````````````````````````````` example
Ping @[Moderators](role:moderator) for help.
.
<p>Ping Moderators for help.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Only @[Admins](role:admin) can do this.
.
<p>Only Admins can do this.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Contact @[Owner](role:owner) for billing.
.
<p>Contact Owner for billing.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Role with a numeric ID in the target.

```````````````````````````````` example
Ping @[Moderators](role:admin:99) for help.
.
<p>Ping Moderators for help.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Alert @[Senior Devs](role:developer:senior:42).
.
<p>Alert Senior Devs.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Multiple role mentions in one sentence.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Admins](role:admin) and @[Mods](role:moderator) please review.
.
<p>Admins and Mods please review.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Both indicators — mixed users, channels, roles

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](user:1) posted in #[announcements](channel:7).
.
<p>Alice posted in announcements.</p>
.
--fmentions
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](user:1) and @[Bob](user:2) join #[general](channel:1).
.
<p>Alice and Bob join general.</p>
.
--fmentions
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Admins](role:admin), please check #[incidents](channel:99).
.
<p>Admins, please check incidents.</p>
.
--fmentions
````````````````````````````````

A dense mix of all three kinds.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](user:1) pinged @[Mods](role:moderator) in #[general](channel:1) and #[off-topic](channel:5).
.
<p>Alice pinged Mods in general and off-topic.</p>
.
--fmentions
````````````````````````````````


## Mention without a target

The parenthesised target is optional.

```````````````````````````````` example
Hello @[John]!
.
<p>Hello John!</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Hello #[general]!
.
<p>Hello general!</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice] and @[Bob] are here.
.
<p>Alice and Bob are here.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Inline formatting inside display text

Emphasis and other inline spans are allowed within the display text.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[**John** Doe](user:1) is bold.
.
<p><strong>John</strong> Doe is bold.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[*italic*](user:2) here.
.
<p><em>italic</em> here.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
#[**important** channel](channel:9)
.
<p><strong>important</strong> channel</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Mentions inside block-level elements

### Headings

```````````````````````````````` example
# Hello @[John](user:1)
.
<h1>Hello John</h1>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
## Channel #[general](channel:1) updates
.
<h2>Channel general updates</h2>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````

### Blockquotes

```````````````````````````````` example
> @[Alice](user:1) said hello.
.
<blockquote>
<p>Alice said hello.</p>
</blockquote>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Nested blockquote.

```````````````````````````````` example
> > @[Alice](user:1) deeply nested
.
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Alice deeply nested</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Lists

Unordered list items.

```````````````````````````````` example
- @[Alice](user:1) reviewed
- @[Bob](user:2) approved
.
<ul>
<li>Alice reviewed</li>
<li>Bob approved</li>
</ul>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Ordered list items.

```````````````````````````````` example
1. @[Alice](user:1) reviewed
2. @[Bob](user:2) approved
.
<ol>
<li>Alice reviewed</li>
<li>Bob approved</li>
</ol>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Channel mentions in a list.

```````````````````````````````` example
- Post in #[general](channel:1)
- Post in #[random](channel:2)
.
<ul>
<li>Post in general</li>
<li>Post in random</li>
</ul>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````

### Tables

Mentions are recognised inside table cells.

```````````````````````````````` example
Name | Role
--- | ---
@[Alice](user:1) | @[Admin](role:admin)
.
<table>
<thead>
<tr>
<th>Name</th>
<th>Role</th>
</tr>
</thead>
<tbody>
<tr>
<td>Alice</td>
<td>Admin</td>
</tr>
</tbody>
</table>
.
--fmention-at --ftables
````````````````````````````````

### Fenced code blocks suppress mentions

Mentions inside fenced code blocks are never recognised — the content is raw
text.

```````````````````````````````` example
```
@[John](user:1)
```
.
<pre><code>@[John](user:1)
</code></pre>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Inline code inside a heading also suppresses the mention.

```````````````````````````````` example
# `@[John](user:1)` is literal
.
<h1><code>@[John](user:1)</code> is literal</h1>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Mention combined with other inline formatting in a heading

```````````````````````````````` example
# **@[Alice](user:1)** is here
.
<h1><strong>Alice</strong> is here</h1>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Code span suppresses mention

A mention inside a code span is not recognised.

```````````````````````````````` example
`@[John](user:1)` is literal.
.
<p><code>@[John](user:1)</code> is literal.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
`#[general](channel:1)` is literal.
.
<p><code>#[general](channel:1)</code> is literal.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Backslash escape suppresses mention

`\@` is a backslash escape that outputs a literal `@`.  The following `[...](...)`
is then a regular CommonMark inline link.

```````````````````````````````` example
\@[John](user:1) is not a mention.
.
<p>@<a href="user:1">John</a> is not a mention.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Malformed mentions fall back to literal text

If there is no closing `]`, the sequence is output as plain text.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[no closer here
.
<p>@[no closer here</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

An empty display text is not recognised as a mention.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[](user:1) is not a mention.
.
<p>@[](user:1) is not a mention.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
#[](channel:1) is not a mention.
.
<p>#[](channel:1) is not a mention.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Bare @ or # are not mentions

A `@` not followed by `[` is never a mention, even with the flag enabled.

```````````````````````````````` example
user@example.com
.
<p>user@example.com</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
Email me @ work.
.
<p>Email me @ work.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

A bare `#` (heading marker in a different context, or standalone hash) is not a
mention.

```````````````````````````````` example
Issue #42 is open.
.
<p>Issue #42 is open.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Mention flag independence

Without `--fmention-hash`, `#[...]` is not a mention indicator.  The `#` is
output as-is and `[name](target)` is a regular CommonMark inline link.

```````````````````````````````` example
#[general](channel:1) without the hash flag.
.
<p>#<a href="channel:1">general</a> without the hash flag.</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

Without `--fmention-at`, `@[...]` is not a mention indicator.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John](user:1) without the at flag.
.
<p>@<a href="user:1">John</a> without the at flag.</p>
.
--fmention-hash
````````````````````````````````


## Edge cases

### Multi-line display text

A soft line break inside the display text is normalised to a space, just like
inside a regular CommonMark link.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John
Doe](user:1)
.
<p>John Doe</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Nested brackets inside display text

The first unresolved `]` always closes the mention (same rule as CommonMark
links).  A `[` inside the display text is treated as a literal character.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John [nick]](user:1)
.
<p>John [nick](user:1)</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Nested parentheses inside the target

The target scanner counts depth, so parentheses nested inside the target string
are kept verbatim.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John](user:(123))
.
<p>John</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](role:(admin:(99)))
.
<p>Alice</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Hard line break immediately after a mention

The trailing spaces that trigger a hard break come after the closing `)`, so
the mention is unaffected.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[Alice](user:1)  
More text
.
<p>Alice<br>More text</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Mention wrapped in emphasis

Emphasis markers outside the mention apply to the display text.

```````````````````````````````` example
*@[John](user:1)*
.
<p><em>John</em></p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Malformed target — no closing parenthesis

If the opening `(` after `]` has no matching `)` before end of line, no target
is consumed.  The `(...)` text is output as literal characters.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John](user:1 no paren
.
<p>John(user:1 no paren</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````

### Target scanner stops at a newline

A target cannot span multiple lines.  A `(` followed by a newline before `)` is
not consumed; the text is output literally.

```````````````````````````````` example
@[John](user:
1)
.
<p>John(user: 1)</p>
.
--fmention-at
````````````````````````````````


## Mention coexists with permissive e-mail autolinks

When both flags are active, `user@domain.com` is still an e-mail autolink and
`@[John]` is still a mention (display text only in HTML output).

```````````````````````````````` example
user@example.com and @[John](user:1).
.
<p><a href="mailto:user@example.com">user@example.com</a> and John.</p>
.
--fmention-at --fpermissive-email-autolinks
````````````````````````````````
